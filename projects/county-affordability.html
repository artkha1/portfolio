County Affordability Analysis

Rigorous applied regression study investigating what county-level factors predict housing cost-to-income ratios across 2,762 U.S. counties — comparing OLS, ridge, and LASSO models with full diagnostics and interaction analysis.

Organization
STAT 420 — Methods of Applied Statistics, UIUC
Category
Statistical Analysis
Tags
RStatistics and Analytics

Background

Completed for STAT 420 (Methods of Applied Statistics) at UIUC. Dataset sourced from Kaggle, containing quality-of-life metrics across U.S. counties compiled from the Census Bureau, EPA, and USDA. The response variable is the mean cost-to-income ratio (AVG C2I) per county as of 2022.

Objective

Investigate which county-level variables most influence housing affordability using rigorous linear regression methodology — comparing multiple model families, diagnosing assumption violations, and arriving at a defensible final model with interpretable coefficients.

Outcome

Final model achieved adjusted R² ≈ 0.51. Key findings: unemployment rate, state fixed effects, and student-to-teacher rank are the strongest predictors. Significant locale × unemployment interaction — the relationship between unemployment and affordability differs substantially between urban and rural counties.
