Sports Spending Analysis

Does money buy success in sports? Scraped and analyzed payroll and transfer data across 7 major leagues (EPL, Serie A, MLS, NFL, MLB, NBA, NHL) to compute a composite success metric and measure Pearson correlation with team spending.

Background

Personally motivated by debates about whether wealth determines success in sports. The project spans 5 different sports and 7 leagues to test whether the relationship holds universally or varies by sport structure and salary cap rules.

Objective

Scrape payroll and transfer spending data for teams across 7 major leagues. Design a composite success metric covering both regular season and playoff performance. Visualize and statistically test the correlation between spending and success in each league.

Outcome

A comparative analysis revealing that the money-to-success relationship varies significantly by league structure — salary cap leagues show weaker correlations than open-market leagues. Findings documented with full methodology in an accompanying report.
