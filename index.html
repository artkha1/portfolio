About Me

Background

I'm a Computer Science student at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, with a focus on data analytics, data engineering, and software development. I enjoy working at the intersection of building things that work and understanding what the data says.

My experience spans software engineering, geospatial analytics, cloud infrastructure, and data-intensive applications. At NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory, I worked on mission support tools and cloud-based data systems for the SWOT satellite mission, contributing to Python development, infrastructure automation, and performance optimization. At University of Chicago Urban Labs, I developed analytical and geospatial workflows for large public-sector datasets, combining data engineering, visualization, and reporting to support research initiatives. Across these experiences, I've become interested in building reliable systems around complex real-world data.

Outside of internships and coursework, I enjoy creating projects that sit somewhere between engineering and experimentation, whether that means building interactive tools, exploring new technologies, or finding cleaner ways to organize and visualize information. I’m particularly drawn to projects where software can simplify complicated processes, support better decisions, or make technical ideas more accessible to people.
